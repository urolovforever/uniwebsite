Exchange Programs

Spend a semester or year abroad at one of our partner universities — broaden your perspective and enrich your degree.

Experience the world while you study.

TIU's exchange programmes give you the opportunity to study at a partner university for one or two semesters while remaining enrolled at TIU. Your credits transfer back to your TIU degree, so you don't lose any time.

Exchange students immerse themselves in a different academic culture, improve their language skills, and gain independence — all qualities that employers value highly. Many of our students describe their exchange semester as the highlight of their university experience.

Our International Office supports you at every step, from choosing a destination to arranging accommodation and visa documents.

Interested in a full joint degree?

If you want to graduate with two diplomas rather than doing a semester exchange, explore our joint degree programmes.

What you get

An exchange at TIU is more than just studying abroad — it's a complete international experience.

Credit transfer

All credits earned at the partner university transfer directly to your TIU degree. No time lost, no repeated courses.

No extra tuition

You continue paying TIU tuition fees during your exchange. Partner university tuition is waived under our agreements.

Full support

Our International Office helps with visa applications, accommodation, travel logistics, and pre-departure orientation.

Cultural immersion

Live in a new country, learn about different cultures first-hand, and develop the intercultural skills employers value.

International network

Build friendships and professional connections with students from around the world that last well beyond graduation.

Career advantage

International experience on your CV sets you apart. Exchange alumni consistently report stronger career outcomes.

Who can apply?

Exchange programmes are open to all TIU students who meet the following criteria:

  • Completed at least one full academic year at TIU
  • Minimum GPA of 3.0 (on a 4.0 scale)
  • English language proficiency (IELTS 6.0+ or equivalent)
  • No outstanding disciplinary issues
  • Approval from your department head
