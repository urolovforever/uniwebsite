Welcome from the Dean

At the Faculty of Jurisprudence, we believe that understanding people, societies, and systems of justice is the foundation for meaningful change in the world.

Our scholars bring together expertise in international law, public law, human rights, and constitutional studies — creating an interdisciplinary environment where students learn to think critically, argue persuasively, and act with purpose.

I'm proud of the community we've built — one that values diverse perspectives, rigorous inquiry, and a genuine commitment to making Central Asia and the wider world a better, more just place.

Research

Research in the Faculty of Jurisprudence is shaping policy, advancing justice, and deepening our understanding of legal systems. We're examining governance frameworks, human rights mechanisms, and constitutional development in Central Asia and beyond.

Our scholars are contributing to vital work in international law, migration studies, comparative law, and legal reform — producing research that influences governments, international organisations, and communities.
