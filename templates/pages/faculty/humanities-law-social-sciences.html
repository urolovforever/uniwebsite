Welcome from the Dean

At the Faculty of Humanities, Law, and Social Sciences, we believe that understanding people, societies, and systems of justice is the foundation for meaningful change in the world.

Our scholars bring together expertise in international law, political science, linguistics, and social sciences — creating an interdisciplinary environment where students learn to think critically, communicate effectively, and act with purpose.

I'm proud of the community we've built — one that values diverse perspectives, rigorous inquiry, and a genuine commitment to making Central Asia and the wider world a better, more just place.

Schools and disciplines

Our Schools bring together scholars across law, humanities, and social sciences — fostering critical thinking, cross-cultural understanding, and the pursuit of justice through world-class teaching and research.

The School of Law offers programmes in international law, public law, and human rights — preparing graduates for careers in legal practice, diplomacy, and policy-making.

The School of Humanities & Social Sciences covers political science, linguistics, psychology, and education — developing thinkers who shape societies.

The Foundation Programme provides academic English, study skills, and subject preparation — giving students a strong start before entering degree programmes.

Political Science & International Relations explores governance, diplomacy, and global affairs — preparing students for careers in policy, international organisations, and public service.

The Linguistics & Languages programme develops expertise in applied linguistics, translation studies, and multilingual communication across Central Asia and beyond.

Education & Pedagogy trains the next generation of educators with modern teaching methodologies, curriculum design, and educational leadership skills.

Research

Research in the Faculty of Humanities, Law, and Social Sciences is shaping policy, advancing justice, and deepening our understanding of societies. We're examining governance systems, human rights frameworks, and the cultural dynamics of Central Asia and beyond.

Our scholars are contributing to vital work in constitutional law, migration studies, applied linguistics, and educational reform — producing research that influences governments, international organisations, and communities.
