{% trans "Admissions" %}

{% trans "You can apply online through our admissions portal. Visit the Apply Now page, select your programme (Bachelor's, Master's, or PhD), fill in the application form, and upload the required documents. Our admissions team will review your application and contact you within 5–10 business days." %}

{% trans "Requirements vary by programme. For Bachelor's programmes, you need a secondary school diploma (attestat) with competitive grades. For Master's programmes, a Bachelor's degree is required. All applicants must demonstrate English proficiency (IELTS 5.5+ or equivalent). Specific programme requirements are listed on each programme page." %}

{% trans "Typically you'll need: a copy of your passport or ID, your most recent diploma/certificate with transcript, an English language proficiency certificate (IELTS, TOEFL, or TIU internal test), a personal statement, and a passport-sized photo. Some programmes may require additional documents such as a portfolio or letters of recommendation." %}

{% trans "TIU accepts applications on a rolling basis. The main intake is in September, with applications typically closing in late August. An early application is recommended as popular programmes may fill up. Check the Admissions page for the exact deadlines for the current academic year." %}

{% trans "Yes, you can apply to up to two programmes simultaneously. If accepted to both, you will choose which offer to accept. There is no additional fee for a second application." %}

{% trans "For most programmes, admission is based on your academic record and English proficiency. Some programmes may include an interview or written assessment. If you don't have an IELTS/TOEFL score, you can take TIU's internal English placement test on campus." %}

{% trans "Most applicants receive a decision within 5–10 business days after submitting a complete application. During peak periods, it may take up to two weeks. You'll be notified by email and can track your application status online." %}

{% trans "Yes, in most cases you can defer your offer by one academic year. Contact the admissions office to request a deferral. Please note that some scholarship offers may not carry over to the next intake." %}

{% trans "Programmes & Study" %}

{% trans "TIU offers Bachelor's, Master's, and PhD programmes across five departments: Business & Economics, Information Technology, Education, Foreign Languages, and Jurisprudence. We also offer a unique 2+2 dual degree programme with international partner universities." %}

{% trans "The primary language of instruction at TIU is English. Some programmes may include components in Uzbek or Russian. All students are expected to have a working level of English by the start of their studies." %}

{% trans "Bachelor's programmes are typically 4 years (or 2+2 with the dual degree option). Master's programmes are 2 years. PhD programmes are 3 years. Part-time options may have different durations depending on the programme." %}

{% trans "Yes, TIU accepts transfer students. Credit transfer is evaluated on a case-by-case basis depending on the courses completed and grades achieved. Contact our admissions office with your transcript for a preliminary assessment." %}

{% trans "Yes. TIU is licensed by the Ministry of Higher Education of Uzbekistan, and our degrees are recognized both nationally and internationally. Our 2+2 dual degree programme means graduates also receive a degree from our partner university abroad." %}

{% trans "Yes, our flagship 2+2 programme allows you to study two years at TIU and two years at a partner university abroad. You graduate with two degrees — one from TIU and one from the partner institution. This is available for select Bachelor's programmes." %}

{% trans "Tuition & Fees" %}

{% trans "Tuition fees vary by programme and level of study. Please visit our Tuition & Fees page for detailed, up-to-date information on all programme costs." %}

{% trans "Yes! TIU offers merit-based and need-based scholarships covering up to 100% of tuition fees. Scholarships are awarded based on academic achievement, entrance exam results, and financial need. Visit our Scholarships page for details and application deadlines." %}

{% trans "Yes, TIU offers flexible payment plans. Tuition can be paid per semester or in monthly instalments. Contact the finance office for payment plan options and deadlines." %}

{% trans "Your tuition covers all academic instruction, access to campus facilities (library, computer labs, sports facilities), student support services, and online learning resources. Housing and meals are not included but are available separately." %}

{% trans "International students may have different fee structures. Please check the Tuition & Fees page or contact our International Admissions office for specific pricing." %}

{% trans "International Students" %}

{% trans "International students typically need a student visa to study in Uzbekistan. Once you receive your official acceptance letter from TIU, our International Office will guide you through the visa application process step by step." %}

{% trans "Yes. Our International Office provides full visa support including invitation letters, document preparation guidance, and liaison with relevant authorities. We help make the process as smooth as possible." %}

{% trans "Yes, we hold a dedicated orientation week for international students before each semester. It includes campus tours, city orientation, registration support, cultural activities, and a chance to meet fellow international students and faculty." %}

{% trans "Most programmes require IELTS 5.5 or equivalent (TOEFL iBT 46+, Cambridge B2). If you don't have an official score, you can take TIU's internal English placement test. Some programmes may have higher requirements — check the specific programme page for details." %}

{% trans "Absolutely. Beyond the 2+2 dual degree programme, TIU has exchange agreements with 10+ partner universities across Europe and Asia. Students can spend a semester abroad while earning credits toward their TIU degree." %}
