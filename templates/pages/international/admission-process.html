simple and straightforward

How to apply in 4 steps

From choosing your programme to arriving in Tashkent

1

Choose your programme

Browse our bachelor's and master's degrees across Business, IT, Education, and Languages — all taught in English

2

Submit your application

Complete the online form and upload your documents: passport, transcripts, English certificate, and motivation letter

3

Receive your offer

Get your admission decision within 2 weeks. Accept the offer and pay the tuition deposit to confirm your place

4

Visa & arrival

Receive your invitation letter, apply for your student visa, and prepare for your journey to Tashkent

What you'll need

Documents & requirements

Prepare the following before starting your application. All documents should be in English, or accompanied by a certified translation.

01
Valid passport With at least 12 months validity remaining from your intended start date
02
Academic certificates & transcripts Secondary school certificate (Bachelor's) or Bachelor's degree (Master's) with full transcript
03
English language certificate IELTS 5.5+ / TOEFL 65+ for Bachelor's, IELTS 6.0+ / TOEFL 78+ for Master's
04
Motivation letter A personal statement explaining why you want to study at TIU and your career goals
05
Passport-size photos 2 recent photos (3.5 x 4.5 cm) on white background
06
Medical certificate Health clearance certificate (required for visa application)

International Applicant FAQs

Most international students require a student visa (type "O'T-2") to study in Uzbekistan. Citizens of some CIS countries may be exempt. TIU's International Office will issue an official invitation letter to support your visa application once you've been accepted.

If you completed your secondary or university education in English, you may be exempt from the language test requirement. Contact our admissions team with your academic documents and we'll confirm your eligibility.

Once you receive your invitation letter from TIU, the student visa is typically processed within 5–10 working days at your local Uzbek embassy or consulate. We recommend applying for your visa at least 4–6 weeks before the semester starts.

Yes. TIU offers student housing options near campus. International students are given priority for university accommodation. We can also help you find private rental options in Tashkent at affordable rates starting from $200/month.

Yes. TIU offers merit-based scholarships covering up to 50% of tuition for high-achieving international applicants. All applicants are automatically considered — no separate scholarship application is needed.

Credit transfer is evaluated on a case-by-case basis. Submit your previous university transcripts along with course descriptions, and our academic office will assess which credits can be transferred to your TIU programme.
