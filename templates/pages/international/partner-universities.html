Partnership FAQs

TIU evaluates potential partners based on academic quality, programme compatibility, student support infrastructure, and shared values. All partnerships are reviewed and approved by the university's International Committee.

Yes! If you know of a university that would be a good fit for TIU, contact the International Office with details. We welcome suggestions from students and faculty and will explore the possibility of establishing a new agreement.

Not all partnerships cover every programme. Exchange availability depends on the specific agreement between TIU and the partner institution. Check with the International Office to see which destinations are available for your field of study.

A dual degree (2+2) programme allows you to study two years at TIU and two years at a partner university, graduating with two internationally recognised qualifications. This is available with select partners in South Korea and Europe.

TIU offers travel grants and exchange bursaries. Some partner universities also provide scholarships or housing support for incoming exchange students. Contact the International Office for details on available funding.
